Introduction to Mathematics

Mathematics is the study of numbers, quantities, and shapes. It is a fundamental science that underlies many other fields.

Branches of Mathematics

  • Algebra: Deals with operations and relations of numbers.
  • Geometry: Studies shapes, sizes, and properties of space.
  • Calculus: Focuses on rates of change and accumulation.
  • Statistics: Analyzes data and makes inferences about populations.
  • Number Theory: Studies properties of integers and prime numbers.
  • Topology: Examines properties of space that are preserved under continuous deformations.

Mathematical Notation

Mathematics uses symbols and notation to represent concepts and relationships. Some common symbols include:

  • \( \pi \): The ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.
  • \( \Sigma \): The sum of a series of numbers.
  • \( \int \): The integral of a function.
  • \( \in \): "Is an element of" or "belongs to."
  • \( \forall \): "For all" or "for every."
  • \( \exists \): "There exists" or "there is at least one."

Mathematical Theorems

A theorem is a statement that has been proven on the basis of previously established statements, such as other theorems, and generally accepted statements, such as axioms.
