Real-World Questions: Needing Statistical Inference

Statistical inference helps us make conclusions from real-world data. Here are some examples where statistical inference is needed:

  1. Does Education Affect Income?
  2. Does Customer Happiness Drive Sales?
  3. Is the Stock Market More Unstable During Recessions?
  4. Does Gravity Vary in Different Locations?
  5. Is the New Drug Better for Lowering Blood Pressure?

Introduction: What is Statistical Inference?

Statistical inference is the process of drawing conclusions about a population based on a sample. It helps us answer questions like: "Is the observed effect real or due to chance?"

Key Concepts

  • Hypothesis Testing
  • Confidence Intervals
  • t-Tests, Chi-Square Tests, and ANOVA

Example Problem: Weight Loss Program

We compare two programs:

  • New Program: Mean weight loss = 7 kg, Standard deviation = 2 kg, Sample size = 20
  • Current Program: Mean weight loss = 5 kg, Standard deviation = 1.5 kg, Sample size = 20

We test if the new program is significantly better.

Step 1: Define Hypotheses

Null Hypothesis: \( H_0: \mu_{\text{new}} = \mu_{\text{current}} \)

Alternative Hypothesis: \( H_1: \mu_{\text{new}} > \mu_{\text{current}} \)

Step 2: Calculate Standard Error (SE)

\[ SE = \sqrt{\left(\frac{S_{\text{new}}^2}{n_{\text{new}}}\right) + \left(\frac{S_{\text{current}}^2}{n_{\text{current}}}\right)} \]

\[ SE = \sqrt{\left(\frac{2^2}{20}\right) + \left(\frac{1.5^2}{20}\right)} = \sqrt{0.2 + 0.1125} = 0.559 \]

Step 3: Compute t-Statistic

\[ t = \frac{(\bar{X}_{\text{new}} - \bar{X}_{\text{current}})}{SE} \]

\[ t = \frac{(7 - 5)}{0.559} = 3.58 \]

Step 4: Compare to Critical Value

With degrees of freedom \( df = 38 \), we check a t-table. If \( p < 0.05 \), we reject \( H_0 \).

Conclusion

Since the p-value is small, we conclude the new program leads to significantly more weight loss.

Example Problem: Chi-Square Test for Independence

We analyze if exercise and sleep quality are related using a contingency table:

Good Sleep Poor Sleep Total
Exercises 30 10 40
Does Not Exercise 20 40 60
Total 50 50 100

We calculate expected values and apply the chi-square formula:

\[ \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O_i - E_i)^2}{E_i} \]

If \( p < 0.05 \), we conclude exercise is related to sleep quality.

Summary

  • Statistical inference helps us determine meaningful patterns in data.
  • We use hypothesis testing and confidence intervals.
  • Tests like t-tests and chi-square help us validate claims.
