Probability Inequalities and Their Practical Applications

1. Markov's Inequality

Formula: \( P(X \geq a) \leq \frac{E[X]}{a} \)

Example: A website records an average visit time of 4 minutes. Markov's inequality tells us that at most 25% of users spend 16 minutes or more on the website.

2. Chebyshev's Inequality

Formula: \( P(|X - \mu| \geq k\sigma) \leq \frac{1}{k^2} \)

Example: In a class where the average score is 70 and standard deviation is 10, at least 75% of students score between 50 and 90.

3. Jensen's Inequality

Formula: \( f(E[X]) \leq E[f(X)] \)

Example: Doubling income does not double happiness. The happiness of an average income is lower than the average happiness of individual incomes.

4. Hoeffding's Inequality

Formula: \( P(|\bar{X} - \mu| \geq t) \leq 2\exp(-2nt^2) \)

Example: Used in election polls to estimate the margin of error.

5. Chernoff Bound

Formula: \( P(X \geq (1+\delta)E[X]) \leq \exp(-\frac{\delta^2 E[X]}{3}) \)

Example: Used in data centers to predict server overload risks.

6. Bernstein's Inequality

Formula: \( P(S_n \geq t) \leq \exp(-\frac{nt^2}{2V + 2Mt/3}) \)

Example: Used in finance to analyze stock market fluctuations.

7. Azuma-Hoeffding Inequality

Formula: \( P\left(\sum X_i - E[\sum X_i] \geq t\right) \leq \exp\left(\frac{-t^2}{2nc^2}\right) \)

Example: Used in fraud detection for online reviews.

8. Talagrand's Concentration Inequality

Formula: \( P(|X - E[X]| \geq t) \leq 2\exp\left(\frac{-t^2}{4\sigma^2}\right) \)

Example: Helps in machine learning to ensure training models generalize well.

10. Bennett's Inequality

Formula: \( P(X \geq t) \leq \exp(-\frac{nt^2}{2V + 2Mt/3}) \)

Example: Used in A/B testing to ensure statistical significance.
